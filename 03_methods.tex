\subsection{Problem Formulation}
We consider online interactive imitation learning with teacher intervention in a
partially observable Markov decision process
$\mathcal{M}=(\mathcal{S},\mathcal{A},\mathcal{O},P,\Omega,r,\gamma)$.
At time $t$, the privileged teacher observes state $s_t\in\mathcal{S}$, whereas
the deployable student receives observation $o_t\in\mathcal{O}$ comprising
stereo RGB images and robot proprioception. The teacher and student produce
$a_t^T\sim\pi_T(\cdot\mid s_t)$ and
$a_t^S\sim\pi_S(\cdot\mid o_t)$, respectively.

A binary gate $g_t\in\{0,1\}$ selects the executed action,
\begin{equation}
a_t=g_t a_t^T+(1-g_t)a_t^S,
\qquad
s_{t+1}\sim P(\cdot\mid s_t,a_t),
\label{eq:gated_action}
\end{equation}
where $g_t=1$ denotes teacher takeover. The induced rollout distribution
$p_{\pi_{\mathcal{G}}}$ therefore changes as the student and intervention gate
$\mathcal{G}$ evolve. VC-DexSafeDAgger combines (i) the SafeDAgger
teacher--student disagreement gate, (ii) a success-labeled Bellman critic, and
(iii) a VLM threshold tuner. Its learning objective and constrained gate are
\begingroup
\small
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\min_{\pi_S,\mathcal{G}}\quad
&-\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim p_{\pi_{\mathcal{G}}}}
[\mathbb{I}_{\mathrm{succ}}(\tau)]
+\lambda_u\mathbb{E}_{\tau\sim p_{\pi_{\mathcal{G}}}}
\!\left[\sum_{t=0}^{T-1}\mathbb{I}_{\mathrm{unsafe}}(s_t,a_t)\right]\\
\text{s.t.}\quad
g_t&=\mathbb{I}\!\left[
\delta_t>\tau_{\delta}^{(k)}
\;\lor\;
\hat Q_{\mathrm{succ}}(s_t,a_t^S)<\tau_Q^{(k)}
\right],\\
\tilde{\boldsymbol{\tau}}^{(k)}
&=\operatorname{VLM}(\mathcal{C}_k),\\
\boldsymbol{\tau}^{(k+1)}
&=(1-\alpha)\boldsymbol{\tau}^{(k)}
+\alpha\,\bar{\boldsymbol{\tau}}^{(k)},\\
\bar{\boldsymbol{\tau}}^{(k)}
&=\operatorname{clip}\!\left(
\tilde{\boldsymbol{\tau}}^{(k)},
\boldsymbol{\tau}_{\min},
\boldsymbol{\tau}_{\max}\right).
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:vc_objective}
\end{equation}
\endgroup
where
$\boldsymbol{\tau}^{(k)}=(\tau_{\delta}^{(k)},\tau_Q^{(k)})$.
$\delta_t$ is the SafeDAgger disagreement score,
$\hat Q_{\mathrm{succ}}$ is the success-value predictor, and
$\mathcal{C}_k$ contains recent rollout statistics and representative visual
states supplied to the VLM. Thus, the VLM tunes the gate thresholds but neither
replaces the critic nor directly selects the executed action.
The unsafe predicate includes harmful collision, object escape, palm flip, and
the hand leaving the valid workspace. Task success requires grasping, lifting,
and holding the object above a target height for a consecutive duration.

\subsection{Overview of the Distillation Pipeline}
VC-DexSafeDAgger has five components:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item a privileged reinforcement-learning teacher;
    \item a stereo RGB student trained from teacher action labels;
    \item a SafeDAgger intervention gate based on teacher--student disagreement;
    \item a learned success-value critic trained from lift-success labels; and
    \item a VLM tuner that calibrates the disagreement and success thresholds.
\end{enumerate}

Training proceeds in two stages. During warm-start, the teacher controls all
environments while its transitions are used to initialize the success critic.
Unsafe warm-start terminal states seed a compact visual buffer for later VLM
calibration. During online distillation, both teacher and student are evaluated
at every step. The teacher action is executed only when the
intervention gate is active; nevertheless, the teacher output remains the
supervision target for the student. The success critic is continually updated
from the resulting gated rollouts. At a much lower frequency, the VLM reviews
aggregated statistics and selected images and proposes conservative threshold
updates. Alg.~\ref{alg:safe_dagger} summarizes the procedure.

\subsection{Privileged Teacher Policy Training}
Teacher learning is formulated as reinforcement learning with transition tuples
$\langle s_t,a_t,r_t,s_{t+1}\rangle$. The objective is
\begin{equation}
\pi_T^*
=
\arg\max_{\pi_T}
\mathbb{E}_{\pi_T}
\left[\sum_{t=0}^{T-1}\gamma^t r_t\right].
\label{eq:teacher_rl_objective}
\end{equation}
We use a shaped reward $r_t=\sum_{k=1}^{K_r}r_{t,k}$ to encourage approach,
stable contact, lifting, and goal tracking (Table~\ref{tab:reward_terms}).
The teacher is trained with an asymmetric actor--critic architecture: its actor
uses noisy robot and object observations, while the critic receives privileged
simulation state.

Episodes terminate at timeout or upon an unsafe event:
\begin{equation}
T=\min\left\{t\;\middle|\;
\mathbb{I}_{\mathrm{unsafe}}(s_t)=1
\;\text{or}\;
t\geq T_{\max}\right\}.
\end{equation}
The hold-gated lift-success condition provides positive supervision for the
critic used during distillation.

\subsection{Risk Predictor}
Action disagreement indicates distribution shift but cannot reliably anticipate
the long-term outcome of locally similar actions. We therefore learn a critic
$Q_{\mathrm{succ}}(s,a)\in[0,1]$ that estimates discounted future lift success
under the rollout policy. The critic is used only during training and is not
part of the deployed student.

We use twin networks $Q_1$ and $Q_2$ with target networks. Each network maps a
state--action pair to a logit $z_i(s,a)$ and produces
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
Q_i(s,a)&=\sigma\!\left(z_i(s,a)/T_Q\right),\\
\hat Q_{\mathrm{succ}}(s,a)&=\min_{i\in\{1,2\}}Q_i(s,a),
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:success_critic}
\end{equation}
where $T_Q$ is an output-calibration temperature. The minimum yields a
conservative success estimate. The input state uses the
predictor-specific transition observation together with the executed action.

The replay buffer stores
$(s_t,a_t,s_{t+1},a_{t+1},u_t,d_t,h_t)$, where $u_t$ is the hold-gated
lift-success label, $d_t$ is the terminal indicator, and $h_t$ indicates whether
a valid next action exists. When success is observed, a short preceding horizon
is also labeled positive. With
$d_t^{\mathrm{eff}}=\max(d_t,1-h_t)$, the SARSA-style target is
\begin{equation}
y_t=
\operatorname{clip}\!\left(
u_t+\gamma(1-d_t^{\mathrm{eff}})
\min_i Q_i^{\mathrm{targ}}(s_{t+1},a_{t+1}),
0,1\right).
\label{eq:success_target}
\end{equation}
The critics minimize
$\mathcal{L}_Q=\sum_{i=1}^{2}
\mathbb{E}[(Q_i(s_t,a_t)-y_t)^2]$, optionally with positive-sample weighting,
and the target parameters are updated using Polyak averaging. After warm-start,
new gated-rollout transitions are appended online so that the critic follows
the evolving student distribution.

\subsection{Intervention Mechanism}
We compute teacher--student disagreement using the teacher action uncertainty
as a per-dimension scale:
\begin{equation}
\delta_t=
\left(
\sum_{j=1}^{d}
\frac{(a_{t,j}^{S}-a_{t,j}^{T})^2}{(\sigma_{t,j}^{T})^2}
\right)^{1/2}.
\label{eq:weighted_disagreement}
\end{equation}
The fast intervention gate combines disagreement with the success critic:
\begin{equation}
g_t=
\mathbb{I}\!\left[
\delta_t>\tau_{\delta}^{(k)}
\;\lor\;
\hat Q_{\mathrm{succ}}(s_t,a_t^S)<\tau_Q^{(k)}
\right],
\label{eq:adaptive_gate}
\end{equation}
where $k$ indexes the latest calibration update. Thus, disagreement catches
student actions that differ sharply from privileged supervision, while the
success critic can intervene when the student action has low predicted long-term
utility even if its instantaneous discrepancy is small. When $g_t=1$, $a_t^T$
replaces the student action for that environment.
Otherwise, the student action is executed. VLM calibration changes only these
thresholds, not the success labels, critic objective, or gate direction.

Within VC-DexSafeDAgger, a vision-language model tunes the two intervention
thresholds at a slower timescale.
Fixed thresholds can become poorly calibrated as the student improves, the
visited state distribution shifts, or different objects induce different
failure modes. VC-DexSafeDAgger treats threshold selection as a slow supervisory
problem while retaining the deterministic gate in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:adaptive_gate}.

At calibration step $k$, the advisor receives a summary
$\mathcal{C}_k$ containing the current thresholds, disagreement and predicted
success distributions, intervention rate, unsafe-episode rate, and proportions of
observed unsafe termination causes. It additionally receives representative
RGB states from a bounded visual buffer. Candidate images are collected from
high-disagreement, low-success, and unsafe-triggered states; sampling is balanced
across objects when multiple object-specific teachers are used. Each image is
paired with metadata including its object identity, disagreement score,
predicted success, current thresholds, and unsafe label.

The VLM is prompted only to recommend
$\tilde{\tau}_{\delta}^{(k)}$ and $\tilde{\tau}_{Q}^{(k)}$, together with a
confidence and short justification. It cannot select actions or set $g_t$
directly. To prevent abrupt or unbounded changes, each proposal is clipped
around a base threshold and exponentially smoothed:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\tau_{x}^{(k+1)}
&=(1-\alpha)\tau_{x}^{(k)}
+\alpha\,\operatorname{clip}\!\left(
\tilde{\tau}_{x}^{(k)},
c_{x}^{\min}\tau_{x}^{0},
c_{x}^{\max}\tau_{x}^{0}\right),\\[-2pt]
&\hspace{5.5em}x\in\{\delta,Q\}.
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:vlm_threshold_update}
\end{equation}
This design separates timescales: the critic and disagreement gate provide
per-step safety decisions, whereas the VLM periodically adjusts their operating
point using broader visual and temporal context through the constrained updates
in Eq.~\eqref{eq:vlm_threshold_update}. If an API call fails or its response
cannot be parsed, the previous thresholds remain unchanged.

\subsection{Student Policy Distillation}
The student consumes stereo RGB images and robot proprioception. Both teacher
and student output Gaussian action distributions with means
$\boldsymbol{\mu}_T,\boldsymbol{\mu}_S$ and standard deviations
$\boldsymbol{\sigma}_T,\boldsymbol{\sigma}_S$. Student learning uses
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{student}}
=
\underbrace{
\sum_{j=1}^{d}
\frac{(\mu_{S,j}-\mu_{T,j})^2}{\sigma_{T,j}^{2}}
+\left\|\boldsymbol{\sigma}_S-\boldsymbol{\sigma}_T\right\|_2^2
}_{\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{action}}}
+
\lambda_{\mathrm{aux}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{aux}},
\label{eq:student_loss}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{aux}}$ supervises visual representations, including
object-position prediction. The student is updated from the current teacher
label at every online step rather than from a student replay buffer. In
contrast, the success critic maintains its own transition replay buffer for
Bellman updates. At deployment, the teacher, success critic, and VLM advisor are
discarded; only the stereo student policy is retained.
