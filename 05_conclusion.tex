\methodname effectively improves exploration safety in a multi-teacher distillation process.
Through extensive evaluation, we validate that the improvement is consistent
across the evaluated object subsets and failure modes. \methodname also appears to approach the teacher policy’s
performance ceiling in avoiding unsafe regions.
Successful real-world deployment further shows that the resulting student policy partially bridges the
sim-to-real gap; however, poor generalization to unseen objects indicates that \methodname is

A clear limitation is that performance during distillation, as well as the final student policy, is
largely constrained by teacher capability. Developing a safety-aware teacher training pipeline is a
promising direction for future work to generate stronger teacher policies.
