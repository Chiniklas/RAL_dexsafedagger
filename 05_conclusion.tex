In this work, we propose a combined intervention mechanism that integrates teacher--student disagreement
with a learned risk predictor.
The proposed method effectively improves exploration safety in a multi-teacher distillation process.
Through extensive evaluation, we validate that the improvement is robust across all test objects and
all failure modes that we consider important. Our method also appears to approach the teacher policy’s 
performance ceiling in avoiding unsafe regions.
Successful real-world deployment further shows that the resulting student policy partially bridges the
sim-to-real gap; however, poor generalization to unseen objects indicates that our pipeline is
vulnerable to unvisited, complex contact scenarios.

A clear limitation is that performance during distillation, as well as the final student policy, is
largely constrained by teacher capability. Developing a safety-aware teacher training pipeline is a
promising direction for future work to generate stronger teacher policies.
